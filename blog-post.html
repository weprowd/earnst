What is GDPR?

GDPR is designed to safeguard individuals' information by regulating how an organisation must store, manage and process data.

Because pf GDPR, consumers have more effective management of their data and how it is used. Consumers will have to consent to use this data and will have the option to request to see any data that a company holds on them. Consumers will also get a "right to forget" under GDPR rules, allowing them to request information about them removed from a company’s records.

For businesses, GDPR means improving security and protection levels over the data that they possess. Organisations will also have to be more upfront when collecting personal data. Data collectors will need to make sure that consent is explicitly given and disclose information on the purpose of the data being collected.

Why is GDPR so necessary?

GDPR is a significant issue for business, no matter the size, whether a multinational or sole trader. The rules significantly affect how to gather, store and manage customer data. If you are an organisation that collects, stores and manages data, the restrictions apply to you.

Is your business GDPR compliant?

It is essential that all organisations that collect, manage and store consumer records operate under the GDPR legislation and the increased control over how that data is used, managed and stored.

As an organisation that manages a customer’s records, you should be able to demonstrate compliance with GDPR through clear and effective policies and agreements and operational procedures and protocols that meet GDPR legislation and have a clear understanding of what’s required of your business as a data controller.
